⚡ Quick Actions

Templates

Manage reusable templates here. This includes trip templates and full site-package templates that can be prepared for individual groups.

Ryder Cup 5 round template

Uses the Myrtle Ryder Cup structure: 5 rounds, seeded team matches, side games, payouts, and the existing Myrtle trip, standings, and prize pages, with roster sizes starting at 12 golfers and staying in groups of 4.

Download Template Guide

Answer these setup questions before the trip is created.

  • What should the trip be called?
  • Where is the trip being played, and what group or organizer name should show on it?
  • What date does round 1 start, and what are the five course names?
  • What are the two Ryder Cup team names?
  • What should the first tee time be, and how many minutes apart should each foursome go off?
  • What package, reservation, coordinator, and contact phone details should be saved?
  • Who are the seeded players in order, and what handicap index should each seed start with? Use at least 12 golfers, in groups of 4.

Group tee-times deployment template

Deploys a dedicated group profile on the shared tee-times stack, then downloads the package spec, guide, and minimal-admin starter artifact for that group.

Download Deployment Guide

Answer these setup questions before the group is deployed and the package files are generated.

This now creates a live shared-stack deployment under a dedicated group slug, with separate public, admin, minimal-admin, calendar, inbound-email alias, and scoped admin/delete codes. A separate database/env per group is still the lower-risk option if you want hard isolation.
  • What should the site be called for this group?
  • What short app name, group name, group reference, and group URL slug should be used?
  • What primary/secondary emails, reply-to email, club-request email, phones, icon, and theme color should ship in the package profile?
  • Which optional modules should be included beyond the core tee-times and admin pages?
  • Any deployment or branding notes that should stay with the package?

Backups

Create a recovery bundle with the primary database, secondary database, and a site snapshot. Run this before major changes and at least monthly.

Monthly Backup Schedule

Uses the existing scheduler. On the configured day/time it creates a full backup bundle and then trims older backups by retention count.

Weekly Backup Schedule

Adds a weekly full backup in addition to monthly retention coverage.

Daily Backup During Active Trip Season

Runs daily only within the selected season window.

Off-Machine Copy Policy

Track whether backups are also copied somewhere outside this server.

Restore Databases

Restore the primary DB, secondary DB, or both from a selected backup. This replaces live data. The confirmation id must exactly match the selected backup.

📬 Scheduled Email Rules

Toggle each scheduled email rule independently. These only apply to automated scheduler runs.

4 PM Club Alert (Tomorrow Empty Tees)

Sends the configured club contact an alert at 4:00 PM if tomorrow has empty tee times.

5 PM Reminder (48 Hours)

Sends subscriber reminder emails for empty tee times 2 days out.

5 PM Reminder (24 Hours)

Sends subscriber reminder emails for empty tee times tomorrow.

5 PM Nearly-Full Alert

Sends subscriber alerts when tee times are over 50% full within 4 days.

Admin Empty-Tee Alerts (6 Hour Cycle)

Sends admin alerts every 6 hours for 24hr/48hr empty tee times.
