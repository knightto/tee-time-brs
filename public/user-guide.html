Tee Time BRS What this app does

Built for golf group captains: fewer texts, fewer mistakes, and a clean, automated sign-up flow.

Why use it?

  • One live calendar for all events — tee times and team (shotgun) formats — so nobody asks “what time are we?”
  • Automatic emails: new events, updates, cancellations, empty-tee reminders, and optional club notifications when you delete a tee time.
  • Frictionless player moves: add, remove, or move golfers with capacity checks; “Maybe” list catches interest without overbooking.
  • Admin clarity: audit log of who changed what and when; admin code required for destructive actions.
  • Extras for your group: Myrtle trip tracking and the 35th Reunion planner live in the same site.

Core workflows

  • Create events fast: Pick course, date, first tee time, and how many times (9-minute spacing). For teams, set team size and start type.
  • Auto-create from email: Send course confirmations to teetime@xenailexou.resend.app (from no-reply@foreupsoftware.com or your Gmail). Subjects like “Tee Time Reservation Confirmation” create events automatically; golfer count ÷ 4 sets tee-time count. Cancellation emails with “Reservation was cancelled” remove the matching event.
  • Manage tee times: Add/remove/rename times; delete with an admin code and optionally notify the club (CC your admin).
  • Manage teams: Shotgun or staggered; auto-names teams; capacity enforced per team size.
  • Players: Add, move between tees/teams, remove, or park on the Maybe list.

Helpful automation

  • Club notification on delete: When you remove a tee time, choose to email the course (and CC your admin) with KNIGHT GROUP TEE TIMES in the subject and clear “please remove from your system” instructions.
  • Course intelligence: Live golf course search with location, phone, website, par/holes; cached for speed.
  • Weather glance: Icons + temps for events within 16 days.

Admin & guardrails

  • Admin code required for destructive actions (event/tee delete) to prevent accidents.
  • Audit log shows who was added/removed/moved and when.
  • Subscriber management: Admin page to manage the list and send reminders.

Special pages you can use

  • Myrtle Trip: Dedicated tracker at /myrtle-trip-2026.html with trip-specific data.
  • Reunion HQ: /reunion.html to manage attendees, logistics, schedule, and planning meetings (PIN-protected edits).

How to onboard your group

  • Share the site link and the access code for your group.
  • Ask golfers to subscribe (top bar) so they get automatic emails instead of texting you.
  • Tell them they can click a date, pick an event, and add themselves—no spreadsheets or reply-all threads.

Daily playbook for captains

  • Create or import the event, confirm course/time count.
  • Watch sign-ups; move players to balance groups; use Maybe for overflow.
  • If the course cancels a time, delete the tee and choose “notify club” so the course and CC get the message.
  • Check audit log if anything looks off; resend reminders from the admin page if a tee is empty.
